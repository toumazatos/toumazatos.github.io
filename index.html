About

I am a PhD student at UMD interested in machine learning security, database systems, and trustworthy AI. Much of my recent work studies how learned components behave under adversarial pressure, especially in settings where they influence system-level decisions such as query optimization or autonomous agent behavior.

Before shifting toward systems security, I also worked on natural language processing for Greek, including transliteration and language tooling. I like research problems that combine theory, measurement, and practical system building.

Research

My current research focuses on the security of machine learning driven systems. A major thread of my work studies learned query optimization, where models influence database execution decisions and therefore create a new attack surface at the boundary between machine learning and traditional systems. I am interested in understanding feature sensitivity, adversarial manipulation, and robustness failures in these settings.

I also work more broadly on trustworthy AI and agentic systems. Across these areas, I care about concrete attacks, careful evaluation, and system-grounded defenses rather than purely abstract threat models.

Learned Query Optimization Security

Studying robustness, feature dependence, adversarial examples, and poisoning effects in learned query optimizers such as Bao.

Agentic AI Security

Interested in prompt injection, attack surfaces in tool-using agents, and the broader security implications of autonomous decision making.

Language Technology

Previous work in NLP for Greek, especially transliteration and practical tooling for low-resource language use.
